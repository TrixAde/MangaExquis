11th Grade Project: Roblox Video Game üéÆ

We presented the game in front of the whole class and our awesome teacher Mr. Deraco ;DD. One of our friends came to demonstrate the game, which was actually in 4 stages. The first stage showed the effects of cyberbullying by allowing the player to place a rose on the grave of a victim, it was titled. Then, the second stage was a long and complex maze (perhaps too long XD), representing the difficult path one has to go through. However, the player had access to a light guiding them, symbolizing the external help that makes the situation easier once we speak up and get support. Afterwards, the third stage was a multiple-choice quiz that asked questions and provided information about cyberbullying. Finally, the fourth and last stage was an escape game: players had to find different letters hidden in several puzzles. These letters formed the word "REPORT", unlocking the final door. Additionally, each completed stage also added the numbers 3, 0, 1, and 8 on a table, which opened a door displaying a final moral and the credits. (3018 is the official anti-cyberbullying helpline number). Everything was going perfectly... maybe a little too perfectly. That‚Äôs when the game crashed right at the end, while the whole class was fully engaged. What a disaster.. I felt SOOO embarrassed, but fortunately, I had asked Yann to record himself playing the game as a backup. We quickly switched to the video, projecting it on the board... and they LOVED IT. I had never been applauded like that in my life. It was an unforgettable moment :'>.
